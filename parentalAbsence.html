What is Parental Absence?

The most common reasons for parental absence include separation, the death of a parent, and parents working abroad. In the Philippines, many parents choose to seek better opportunities by working overseas rather than staying with their families. While this may help address financial problems, it often leaves children behind—those who are most affected and who bear the greatest emotional and social consequences. Parental absence can have adverse effects on a child’s overall well-being, decision-making, behavior, and coping strategies. Without proper parental guidance, children are more likely to develop high rates of alcoholism, perform poorly in school, and engage in dangerous activities. These consequences often appear as challenges in their academic, social, and emotional well-being.
