Coping Mechanisms Employed by OFW Children

Coping Mechanisms

Coping Through Hobbies and
Social Connection


To manage the emotional challenges of parental separation, many OFW children engage in hobbies such as watching anime, dancing, reading, rapping, or playing sports. These activities serve as healthy distractions that help them release stress and avoid dwelling on loneliness. Chen and Qu (2021) explain that coping strategies are generally divided into approach coping (seeking emotional support from others) and avoidant coping (withdrawing, denying emotions, or self-blame). Many OFW children use approach coping by turning to friends and relatives for comfort and guidance. Basco et al. (2025) also found that family members often fill the emotional gap left by absent parents, offering stability and someone to rely on during difficult moments.
